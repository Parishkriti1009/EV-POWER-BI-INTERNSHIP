# EV Adoption vs Charging Infrastructure Dashboard

## Problem Statement
The adoption of Electric Vehicles (EVs) in India has been increasing rapidly, particularly in segments such as electric rickshaws, two-wheelers, and small commercial vehicles. These vehicles are gaining popularity because they are cost-effective, environmentally friendly, and help reduce urban air pollution and carbon emissions. Electric rickshaws, in particular, have become a major mode of last-mile transportation in many Indian cities, providing sustainable mobility while also creating employment opportunities.

However, despite the rapid growth in EV adoption, the development of charging infrastructure has not kept pace. Many regions have a limited number of public charging stations, which creates a significant gap between the number of electric vehicles on the road and the available charging facilities. This infrastructure gap can lead to range anxiety for users, operational challenges for commercial EV drivers, and slower adoption of electric mobility in the long term.

This project analyzes the relationship between EV adoption and charging infrastructure across different states in India to identify regions where this gap is most significant and where infrastructure expansion is needed.

## Project Overview
This Power BI dashboard analyzes the gap between Electric Vehicle (EV) adoption and charging infrastructure across Indian states.

## Objectives
- Analyze EV adoption trends
- Identify states with infrastructure gaps
- Provide insights for policy and planning

## Tools Used
- Power BI
- Excel
- Data Cleaning
- Data Visualization

## Key Insights
- EV adoption is increasing rapidly in several states.
- Charging infrastructure growth is slower compared to EV adoption.
- Some states show a significant gap between EV demand and charging stations.
